Formulas you need to know

Transformer voltage ratio (7.19P)
Vp / Vs = np / ns
Vp = primary voltage, Vs = secondary voltage, np = primary turns, ns = secondary turns
Transformer power (100% efficiency) (7.20P)
Vp × Ip = Vs × Is
Input power = output power. Ip = primary current, Is = secondary current
Units you must use (7.1)
A, V, W
Ampere (current), Volt (voltage/EMF), Watt (power)

Key word definitions

Magnetically hard — material that is difficult to magnetise but keeps its magnetism (e.g. steel). Used for permanent magnets.
Magnetically soft — material that is easy to magnetise and loses its magnetism quickly (e.g. iron). Used for electromagnets.
Induced magnetism — when a magnetic material is placed in a field and becomes a temporary magnet itself.
Solenoid — a coil of wire; when current flows it produces a field like a bar magnet.
Fleming's left-hand rule — thumb = force (motion), index = field, middle finger = current. Gives direction of force on a wire.
Electromagnetic induction — creating a voltage by moving a conductor through a magnetic field or changing the field through a coil.
Step-up transformer — increases voltage (more turns on secondary). Used to transmit electricity at high voltage.
Step-down transformer — decreases voltage (fewer turns on secondary). Used to make electricity safe for homes.
